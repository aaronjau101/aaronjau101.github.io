About Aaron Jauregui

Who is this guy?

Short story long, my life story:


Well, I'd say it all starts with 6 year old me playing Mario Kart 64 in my cousin's basement. That Christmas, I begged my parents for a Gameboy Color and they complied. Little did they know, they unknowingly signed a binding contract to purchase thousands of dollars worth of video games.

Nintendo 64

Runescape Gold

Fast forward to middle school a.k.a. the beginning of my juvenile record. I discover Runescape with my friends. We instantly begin scamming people for their hard-earned gold. I try using programs online to edit my in-game money to no avail. To this day, I'm pretty sure my friends and I are the reason new security measures were put in place to Runescape.


High school begins. The amount of time I played Call of Duty or Halo is more than twice the amount of the time I spend studying. My senior year, I take a computer class where I first get my feet wet in coding. We use ActionScript to create these small Flash animations. I spend hours creating an animation, that had nothing to do with a school assignment, to create something I could laugh at with my friends for a couple minutes. This was the start of something huge...

Mac Aaron Photoshop

Go Slugs!

UC Santa Cruz, the punch of reality into my fragile face. My first few months, I learned Javascript using the Processing library to make some cute drawings and games. UCSC: "Oh, so you like coding do you? Here's C and Java". Cue the countless hours of me looking at broken code with nothing but existential questions in my head. I survived my way past the programming fundamentals and made it to the motherland. Unity and Unreal! I graduate with a Bachelors of Science in Computer Science: Game Design.


Present Day. I am a Coding Instructor teaching my future competition. I spend my free time creating computer games using Unity and Javascript. I regained my love for Runescape (this time for moral reasons) and also dabble with a variety of Steam games. My dream job is to get a steady pay creating video games in my pajamas. My hope is that my career will one day inspire a kid playing my game in his cousin's basement to follow in my footsteps.
